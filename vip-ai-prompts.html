Prove your niche before you build. Nail your messaging before you sell.

PROMPT 01

Niche Validation Analyzer

Use BEFORE you build anything. If the AI gives you below a 7, pivot your niche or angle.

PROMPT 01 - NICHE VALIDATION
You are a digital product market researcher who specializes in low-ticket digital products ($27-$97). I want to validate a product idea before I build anything. MY NICHE: [TARGET NICHE - e.g., "busy moms who want to meal prep"] MY EXPERTISE: [YOUR BACKGROUND - e.g., "certified nutritionist, 5 years coaching"] MY IDEAL BUYER: [DESCRIBE THEM - age, income, what they struggle with] Analyze this niche using the following framework: CONTEXT: This analysis is for a low-ticket digital product ($27-$97) sold primarily through organic traffic (YouTube, social media, email). The business model follows Marissa Romero's course funnel architecture where a $47 front-end product converts at ~7% and feeds into an upsell funnel that 2.5x the revenue. The goal is NOT mass market - it's micro-niche with high intent buyers. (1) DEMAND SCORE (1-10): Is there active demand for PAID solutions? Look for existing courses, templates, paid communities in this space. If people only consume free content, score low. (2) MICRO-RESULT POTENTIAL: What specific, measurable results can I promise in days (not months)? Give me 5 "micro-result" options that would make someone screenshot the sales page. (3) COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE: Top 5 competing products with prices. Where are the gaps? (4) "WITHOUT" ANGLE: What outdated advice or painful methods does this audience hate? Give me 5 "without [thing they've tried]" options. (5) BUYER READINESS: Has this audience already spent money on this problem? What's their buying history? (6) ECOSYSTEM POTENTIAL: Could a $47 buyer eventually become a $3K-$10K client? What would that ascension path look like? (7) OVERALL VIABILITY: Rate 1-10 with detailed reasoning. If viability is below 7, suggest 3 specific angle pivots that would increase it to 8+.
Marissa's benchmark: She made $3,978 from just 700 visitors and 56 buyers on a small YouTube channel with ~150 subscribers. You don't need a huge audience - you need the right niche.

PROMPT 02 - MESSAGING FORMULA TESTER
You are a direct-response copywriting expert who specializes in low-ticket digital product messaging. I'm using Marissa Romero's messaging formula: "The [Blueprint/System/Framework] to [Micro Result] Without [Outdated Advice]" Here is my messaging formula: "[PASTE YOUR FORMULA HERE]" My niche: [NICHE] My product price: [PRICE] My audience: [WHO THEY ARE + THEIR BIGGEST FRUSTRATION] CONTEXT ON THE FORMULA: - The [Blueprint/System/Framework] part signals structure and proves it's not just "tips." It should feel proprietary, like something only YOU teach. It builds instant confidence. - The [Micro Result] must be SPECIFIC and achievable in days, not months. Bad: "grow your business." Good: "book 3 clients this week." The more measurable and time-bound, the more someone will screenshot it. - The [Without] creates instant trust because you UNDERSTAND their frustration. It should name what they've already tried that failed. Bad: "without stress." Good: "without posting every day." Evaluate my formula on: (1) CLARITY (1-10): Would a stranger scrolling Instagram instantly understand what they get? (2) SPECIFICITY (1-10): Is the micro-result measurable? Can someone picture the outcome? (3) DIFFERENTIATION (1-10): Does the "without" feel fresh and personal, not generic? (4) SCREENSHOT TEST (1-10): Would someone screenshot this and send it to a friend saying "I need this"? (5) OVERALL SCORE with explanation Then give me 5 improved versions, ranked strongest to weakest. For each, explain what you changed and why it's stronger. Finally: which of my original + your 5 versions would you bet money on for a $47 product sold through a YouTube video description link?
Example from Marissa: "The Nervous System Reset Framework to sleep through the night without waking at 1am - without having to meditate for an hour." Notice how specific the result and the "without" are.

PROMPT 03 - AUDIENCE DEEP-DIVE VIP ONLY

CONTEXT: This product is sold via organic traffic (YouTube, Instagram, email list). The buyer sees a piece of content, clicks a link, lands on a sales page, and buys in one session. There's no sales call. The messaging has to do ALL the selling. That means I need to know EXACTLY how my buyer talks about their problem - not how I talk about it. Create a comprehensive buyer profile: DEMOGRAPHICS & HABITAT: - Age range, income level, life stage - Where they hang out online (specific subreddits, Facebook groups, hashtags, podcasts) - What YouTube channels/creators they already follow in this space - What free content have they already consumed on this topic? PSYCHOGRAPHICS (THE DEEP STUFF): - Their #1 frustration RIGHT NOW, written in their own words (not clinical language) - What have they already tried that didn't work? (at least 5 failed attempts) - What does "success" look like to them? Be hyper-specific (not "feel better" but "wake up without dreading the day") - Their secret fear about this topic that they wouldn't say out loud - The exact phrase they'd type into Google at 11pm when they can't sleep - What would they DM a friend about this problem? BUYING TRIGGERS & OBJECTIONS: - What digital products have they already purchased? (price range, what worked, what didn't) - What would make them buy impulsively vs. "I'll think about it"? - Top 5 objections to buying another digital product about this topic - The trigger event that makes them ready to buy NOW (life change, frustration peak, deadline) VOICE OF CUSTOMER: Give me 10 "voice of customer" quotes - written EXACTLY how this person would talk to a friend, including slang, frustration, and emotion. These should be usable as: - Sales page headlines - Email subject lines - Social media hooks - Ad copy angles

PROMPT 04 - PRODUCT BLUEPRINT
You are a digital product architect. I want to create a [PRODUCT TYPE: mini course / template pack / workbook / quiz] priced at $[PRICE]. My niche: [NICHE] My messaging formula: "[YOUR FORMULA]" My audience's #1 problem: [PROBLEM] CONTEXT: This product follows Marissa Romero's course funnel architecture. Key principles: - The product delivers ONE micro-result, not a complete life transformation - Every module needs a TANGIBLE deliverable (template, script, checklist, tracker) - not just "access to a video" - The buyer should get a quick win in the first 15 minutes - Product completion time should be under 2 hours (shorter = higher completion = happier buyers = more referrals) - The product itself acts as a sales call for higher-ticket offers ($3K-$10K coaching/services) - 3 out of 56 buyers in Marissa's case study became $10K-$20K high-ticket clients - Product types that work: mini course (5-7 videos, 10-15 min each), template pack (done-for-you resources), workbook/guide (PDF with exercises), quiz/assessment (diagnostic + action plan) Create a complete product blueprint: (1) PRODUCT NAME - give me 7 options using power words (blueprint, system, vault, toolkit, accelerator, playbook, formula). Each name should pass the "would I click this?" test. (2) QUICK WIN - what result do they get in the FIRST 15 minutes? This is critical for momentum. (3) MODULES/SECTIONS - outline 3-5 modules with: - Benefit-driven title (not "Module 1: Introduction") - What they learn (1-2 sentences) - Tangible deliverable (template, checklist, script, worksheet) - Time to complete (4) BONUSES (3) - each should have a name, description, and perceived value ($50-$200). These should cost me $0 to create but feel incredibly valuable. (5) "WHO THIS IS FOR" - 5 statements: "This is for you if..." (6) "WHO THIS IS NOT FOR" - 3 statements (builds trust through exclusion) (7) TRANSFORMATION STATEMENT: "You'll go from ___ to ___ in ___ days" (8) ECOSYSTEM PATH: How does this $[PRICE] product naturally lead to a higher-ticket offer? What would the $97 upsell be? The $297 upsell?
Marissa's rule: if your product has more than 5 modules, you're over-teaching. Shorter courses = higher completion = happier customers = more referrals and ascension.

PROMPT 07 - LAUNCH EMAIL SEQUENCE
You are an email copywriter for course creators. Write a 5-email launch sequence. PRODUCT: "[PRODUCT NAME]" - $[PRICE] AUDIENCE: [WHO THEY ARE] CORE PROMISE: [MESSAGING FORMULA] LAUNCH DATES: [CART OPEN DATE - CART CLOSE DATE] MY STORY: [1-2 SENTENCES: why you created this, what personal experience drives it] CONTEXT: These emails will be loaded into GoHighLevel as an automated workflow triggered when cart opens. The list is warm (they've consumed free content or attended a challenge). This is NOT cold email - these people already know who I am. Each email should be under 300 words, use short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max), and have ONE clear CTA. EMAIL FRAMEWORK (Marissa's 5-email launch structure): Email 1: ANNOUNCEMENT - "It's here." Excitement + exactly what they get. Link to sales page. Email 2: SOCIAL PROOF - "[X] people already grabbed this." Early buyer reactions, answer 2-3 FAQs. Email 3: OBJECTION CRUSHER - "But will this work for me?" Address the #1 hesitation head-on with empathy and evidence. Email 4: DEADLINE + BONUS - "This disappears in 24 hours." Add a time-limited bonus. Create urgency without being scammy. Email 5: LAST CHANCE - Use the "3 Options" framework: Option 1 (do nothing, nothing changes), Option 2 (figure it out yourself, takes longer), Option 3 (let me help you, fastest path). Honest, direct close. For EACH email, give me: - Subject line + 2 alternatives - Preview text (the text that shows after the subject in inbox) - Full email body (under 300 words) - CTA button text - Best send time (morning/afternoon/evening) with reasoning Tone: Like a friend who happens to have the solution. Not salesy. Not corporate. Real.
The "3 Options" framework in Email 5 is Marissa's most powerful closer. It works because it's honest - you're not pressuring, you're presenting reality.
